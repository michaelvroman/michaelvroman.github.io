More about me

I am not a typical traveler who wants to see some of the beautiful places around the world for leisure like Europe.

For the past 20 years, I have volunteered to travel and work to some of the isolated and even dangerous parts of the world such as Djibouti and Kyrgyzstan.

One of my favorite memories is interacting with one of the protected species in Diego Garcia Island which is the giant coconut crab.

There is nothing to really do here on the island except maybe fishing which I used to love. And love to eat them after catch.

Diego Garcia is a remote island located in the middle of the Indian Ocean. It is owned by the British and also called the British Indian Ocean Territory which consists of several small islands.

The coconut crab you are seeing in the picture is actually the largest terrestrial arthropod in the world, meaning that it is in the same category or species of the smallest arthropods like parasitic wasps and beetles.

Military Background

I served in the military for over 10 years, but I was involuntarily separated due to force reduction.

One of my least favorite photos is the picture of myself wearing 7 layers of clothing on top of the remote hill in Afghanistan. Nothing is compared to the fantastic weather of San Diego. I volunteered to work in Kabul, Afghanistan for almost a year as an executive assistant for the military unit.
As of 2021, Afghanistan is still ranked as the most dangerous country in the world.

One that I found interesting while I worked with the Afghan translators was the way they eat food. The rule is only the right hand should be used when eating with your hands.
